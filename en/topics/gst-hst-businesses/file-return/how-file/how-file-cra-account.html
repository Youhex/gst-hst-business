Filing in your CRA account (using NETFILE) – File your GST/HST return

You can file your GST/HST return and eligible rebates directly to the CRA by logging into your CRA account (My Business Account or Represent a Client).

If your return is created and you are ready to file, proceed to:
Sign in to your account to file your return

On this page

  • What you can and cannot do

  • How to file
  • Notices

What you can and cannot do

Most GST/HST registrants can file a regular return using this service.

You can:

  • Meet electronic filing requirements
  • Get a confirmation number right away
  • File without an GST/HST access code
  • File elections
  • File a combined GST/HST and QST return for selected listed financial institutions (form RC7200)
  • File a return for a non-resident
  • File a simplified GST/HST return for a non-resident digital economy business
  • Include schedules
  • File Information on Claims Paid or Credited for Foreign Conventions and Tour Packages (form GST106)
  • File certain rebates with your return (refer to Claim a GST/HST rebate):
    • Application for GST/HST Public Service Bodies' Rebate and Self-Government Refund (form GST66)
    • General GST/HST rebate application (form GST189 – reason codes 1A, 1C, 5, 7, 8, 9, 12, 13, 16, 20, 23, 24, or 25
    • New Housing Rebate Application for Houses Purchased from a Builder (form GST190)
    • GST/HST Rebate Application for Federal, Provincial, and Territorial Governments (form GST490)
    • Non-profit Organizations – Government Funding (form GST523-1)
    • New Residential Rental Property Rebate Application (form GST524)

Filing a GST/HST return for a non-resident in your CRA account

You must:

  • Have a Social Insurance Number (SIN) or have an authorised representative in Canada
  • Complete all GST/HST amounts in Canadian dollars
  • Remit Canadian funds using a participating financial institution in Canada

You cannot:

  • File without an online CRA account
  • File a return for an account administered by Revenu Québec
  • File rebates not listed above (refer to Claim a GST/HST rebate)

If you want to use your online CRA account for your return but need to file other rebate forms, include the rebate amount on your electronic return and mail the application form to the applicable tax centre. Make sure the tax centre receives the form on or before the due date of your return.

If you cannot file what you need to using this method, or cannot get a CRA account, you can choose another filing method that works for you.

Before you start

If you plan to file in your CRA account, make sure you:

  • Are able to sign in to your CRA account
  • Know what you can and cannot file using this method
  • Read the security, privacy and non-responsibility statements under Notices
  • Are aware of the hours of service for CRA electronic services
  • Have the necessary web browser and browser settings
  • You have your Business Number (BN)
  • You have your reporting period "from" and "to" dates

How to file

  1. Prepare your return information in Canadian funds
  2. Enter your return information into the GST/HST NETFILE form by following the instructions on the application pages.
    • Your secure session will expire after 35 minutes of inactivity
    • If you end your filing session before confirming that you want to file your return, the information you entered will not be saved

    File your return using the GST/HST NETFILE form

  3. After you successfully submit your return you will receive a confirmation number. This confirmation number is your proof that the CRA has received your return. Print the page with your confirmation number and keep it with your records.
  4. After you exit the online forms, it is recommended you shut down and restart your browser. This will help ensure that any information you entered cannot be viewed by others.
  5. Depending on whether you expect to make a payment or receive a refund, review the following information:
    1. If you have an amount owing, find out how to make a payment to the CRA (or proceed to pay - MyBA only?)
    2. Consider setting up direct deposit to speed up any refund you may be expecting

What's new

  • The way you report the net tax calculation changed as of May 13, 2024. Lines 105 and 108 are automatically calculated based on the amounts you enter on lines 103, 104, 106, and 107.
  • If you have no amounts to report you can file your return faster by selecting “I am filing a nil return (all fields are $0)” on the first Web form. Your return will then have $0.00 entered in all the fields. To file your return, agree to the certification statement, and select “Submit”.
  • Public service bodies can file Form GST66, Application for GST/HST Public Service Bodies’ Rebate and GST Self-Government Refund, and Form GST523-1, Non-profit Organizations – Government Funding, using the links on the Electronic rebate forms page.
  • You can sign up for email notifications from the CRA by providing your email address. We will then send you an email when notices, letters, and statements are available to be viewed in My Business Account instead of mailing you a paper copy. (Separate registration for My Business Account is required).

Notices

Security and confidentiality

The CRA limits access to browsers supporting Transport Layer Security (TLS). TLS is a protocol that provides authentication, confidentially, and data integrity between communicating applications. Using the appropriate safeguards and configuration, TLS provides a method of transmitting data securely across networks. Effective implementation of TLS will assist in mitigating the most serious threats with web-based applications such as eavesdropping, data tampering, and message forgery. Upgrade to TLS 1.2 so that your system is as secure as possible.

The CRA uses advanced encryption technology and security protocols to protect your personal information. However, while we have made every possible effort to ensure the safety and integrity of transactions at our Web site, the Internet is a public network and therefore is outside our control.

Privacy Notice Statement

Personal information is collected under the authority of the Excise Tax Act and/or Québec Sales Tax Act (QSTA) for the purpose of filing your goods and services tax/ harmonized sales tax (GST/HST) and/or Québec sales tax (QST) returns and claiming eligible rebates. Please note that the Internet Protocol (IP) address will be collected, stored and used to identify the general location from where a return/rebate was filed from, to identify anomalies and/or inconsistencies that may warrant further review.

Information may also be used for the administration and enforcement of the ETA and/or QSTA and may be shared or verified with other government institutions as permitted by law. Incomplete or inaccurate information may result in the delay or denial of refunds and/or rebates; penalties may also apply.

Information is described in personal information bank PPU 241 in the CRA chapter of Information about Programs and Information Holdings (formerly Info Source). Personal information is protected under the Privacy Act and individuals have a right of protection, access to and correction or notation of their personal information. To view the CRA Information about Programs and Information Holdings (formerly Info Source) chapter and further details regarding requests for personal information at the CRA, go to Access to information and privacy at the CRA.

Regarding information relative to the QST, its protection and access provisions are also covered in the Tax Administration Act and in the Access to Documents Held by Public Bodies and the Protection of Personal Information Act.

Non-responsibility of the CRA

The CRA has taken all reasonable steps to ensure the security of this Web site. We have used sophisticated encryption technology and incorporated other procedures to protect your personal information at all times. However, the Internet is a public network and there is the remote possibility of data security violations. In the event of such occurrences, the CRA is not responsible for any damages you may experience as a result.
