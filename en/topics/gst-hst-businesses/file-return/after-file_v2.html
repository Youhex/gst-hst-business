What happens after you file

Once we process your GST/HST return, we will send you a notice of assessment, only if one of the following statements is true:

  • You are owed a refund or rebate
  • The amount you owe is more than what you paid

If you still owe money, we will also send you a remittance voucher (a payment slip) so you can pay your balance (note section on this page re paying) .

Notice of assessment

If we send you a notice of assessment and you are signed up for email notifications, we will email you to let you know there is mail in your online CRA account. If you are not signed up, we will mail you a paper copy.

Your notice of assessment may include details about penalties and interest if you owe money. To learn more, visit GST/HST – Penalties and interest

When to expect your refund

You can claim a refund if your net tax (line 109 of your GST/HST return) is a negative amount.

In most cases, we process GST/HST returns within 4 weeks.

Your refund may be delayed if:

  • You did not include all necessary information
  • You did not complete your return correctly

If you signed up for direct deposit, we will deposit your refund directly into your bank account. To learn more, visit Direct deposit – Payments the CRA sends you.

If you are owed interest on your refund, we will pay it based on the prescribed interest rate. Interest is compounded daily on an overpayment up to and including the day the overpayment is refunded or applied to another debt.

Refund holds

If you have any missing returns or unpaid amounts under other acts administered by the CRA, we can hold your GST/HST refund or rebate until you have filed your required returns and paid your outstanding amounts.

These acts include:

  • Excise Tax Act
  • Excise Act 2001
  • Income Tax Act
  • Air Travellers Security Charge Act

If you are a sole proprietor or part of a partnership, we will also hold your personal income tax refund.

We can also use any GST/HST refund or rebate that you are entitled to receive to pay your outstanding amount.

Paying your balance

If you owe money, we will also send you a personalized remittance slip that you can use to pay your balance: Form RC159, Remittance Voucher – Amount Owing.

Form RC159 is not available electronically. You can order a personalized printed copy by  signing in to your CRA online account and accessing your business or your representive services .

Checking a previous return

To view the status and the details of a previously filed GST/HST return, sign in to your online CRA account to access My Business Account or Represent a client.

You can also view upcoming or overdue GST/HST returns by using the same digital services.

Fixing mistakes on a return

Changing a return you already file

If you made a mistake on a GST/HST return you already filed, do not file a new return. Instead you must submit an adjustment to your filed return in one of the folowing ways:

  • Make an adjustment in your CRA account: Option 1

    • Sign in to your online CRA account
    • Access My Business Account or Represent a Client
    • Make the necessary changes to your return

  • Mail us adjustment information: Option 2

    Send a letter to your tax centre with:

    • Your GST/HST business number
    • The reporting period you need to correct
    • The corrected amounts for each line on your return
    • The name and phone number of someone we can contact if needed

    The letter must be signed by the business owner or an authorized representative.

Make a voluntary disclosure

The Voluntary Disclosures Program (VDP) allows you to:

  • Correct incorrect or missing information
  • Report information you did not include in a previous return

You may apply to the VDP to be granted relief on applicable penalties and prosecution. If you qualify, you will only have to pay the tax owed and any interest.

To learn more, refer to: Voluntary Disclosures Program

Prepare for reviews and audits

What you need to keep

When you file electronically, you should keep a copy of your return and any other information you used to prepare it. These records are subject to audit and must be retained for verification purposes.

To learn more about what other documents you need to keep, refer to: GST/HST records to keep

How to prepare for a post-assessment review

We may contact you for a post-assessment review to verify the amounts on your return. This is not an audit.

During this review, we may ask you to:

  • Explain the amounts reported on your return
  • Provide supporting documents like receipts or invoices

If needed, we will make adjustments to your return.

How to prepare for an audit

A tax audit can be overwhelming. Asking yourself the following questions may help you prepare for an audit :

  • Why do we audit and what is a tax audit?
  • What are your responsibilities and what happens during an audit?
  • What are your rights?

To answer these questions, refer to: Business audits
