Most GST/HST registrants can file their return electronically on the Canada Revenue Agency website via the use of CRA-approved third party software. This online service is also reffered to as Internet File Transfer and requires the use a four-digit access code for authentication.

If your return is created and you are ready to file, click continue to proceed.

Eligibility

Filing using Internet File Transfer is available to most GST/HST registrants, excluding those with accounts that are administered by Revenu Québec. If you are a selected listed financial institution (SLFI) that reports both the GST/HST and QST, you must determine which returns to complete and when to file.

The following registrants are not eligible to use GST/HST Internet File Transfer and must use an alternate filing method:

  • Registrants whose accounts are administered by Revenu Québec
  • Builders required to report transitional information on housing (Schedule A of the GST/HST return)
    • These filers must use GST/HST NETFILE
  • Registrants required to recapture input tax credits for the provincial portion of the HST on specified property or services (Schedules B and C of the GST/HST return)
    • These filers must use GST/HST NETFILE or file through a participating financial institution that offers electronic filing for GST/HST returns

Before you start

Before using GST/HST Internet File transfer, ensure you meet the following requirements:

  1. You are eligible to use GST/HST Internet File transfer
  2. You have read the Privacy Notice Statement and the Statement of non-responsibility
  3. You are using a browser which supports TLS 1.2
    • Note: If you are using an older browser that does not support the latest security standard, you will not be able to access CRA's secure online services
  4. You have enabled cookies and JavaScript in your browser
  5. You have your four-digit access code

How it works

Using CRA-approved third party software, you generate and save an electronic version of your GST/HST return. You then access the service through the CRA application page and attach your (.tax) file.

The application does a preliminary check of your return. If the return meets the basic requirements, you'll receive your confirmation number. This means that your tax return has been accepted for processing. Keep this confirmation number for your records.

If there are any problems with the electronic tax return you tried to send us, we'll send you an explanation of errors or corrections needed. This means your tax return was not accepted for processing. You should save, print, or make note of all messages we send. Before trying to retransmit the return, you must make the appropriate corrections within your software according to the error messages you received and re-save your file.

Using the service

  1. To use GST/HST GST/HST Internet File transfer to file your return, create and save your return using CRA-approved third party software.
    • Prepare your return information in Canadian funds.
    • Verify that the Business Number, reporting period and return information generated by your software is correct.
  2. Record the file name and path of the (.tax) file containing your electronic GST/HST return.
  3. If you have an amount owing, find out how to make a payment to the CRA.
  4. When you are ready to file, click on the "Continue" button below to access the GST/HST Internet File transfer application.
  5. Follow the instructions on the application page to upload your file.
    • If you end your filing session before confirming that you want to file your return, the information you entered will not be saved.
    • Your secure session will expire after 35 minutes of inactivity.
  6. Once you have attached the file, you should ensure that your return is correct, complete and accurate.
  7. Confirm that you want to file the information as your return by selecting "yes" to the declaration.
  8. After you successfully submit your return you will receive a confirmation number.
    This confirmation number is your proof that the CRA has received your return. Print the page with your confirmation number and keep it with your records.
  9. Consider setting up direct deposit to speed up any refund you may be expecting.

Notices

Security and confidentiality

The CRA limits access to browsers supporting Transport Layer Security (TLS). TLS is a protocol that provides authentication, confidentially, and data integrity between communicating applications. Using the appropriate safeguards and configuration, TLS provides a method of transmitting data securely across networks. Effective implementation of TLS will assist in mitigating the most serious threats with web-based applications such as eavesdropping, data tampering, and message forgery. Upgrade to TLS 1.2 so that your system is as secure as possible.

Privacy Notice Statement

Personal information is collected under the authority of the Excise Tax Act for the purpose of filing your goods and services tax/ harmonized sales tax (GST/HST) returns and claiming eligible rebates. Please note that the Internet Protocol (IP) address will be collected, stored and used to identify the general location from where a return/rebate was filed from, to identify anomalies and/or inconsistencies that may warrant further review.

Information may also be used for the administration and enforcement of the ETA and may be shared or verified with other government institutions as permitted by law. Incomplete or inaccurate information may result in the delay or denial of refunds and/or rebates; penalties may also apply.

Information is described in personal information bank PPU 241 in the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) chapter of Programs and Information Holdings (formerly Info Source). Personal information is protected under the Privacy Act and individuals have a right of protection, access to and correction or notation of their personal information. To view the CRA Programs and Information Holdings (formerly Info Source) chapter and further details regarding requests for personal information at the CRA, go to Access to information and privacy at the Canada Revenue Agency.

Non-responsibility of the CRA

The CRA has taken all reasonable steps to ensure the security of this Web site. We have used sophisticated encryption technology and incorporated other procedures to protect your personal information at all times. However, the Internet is a public network and there is the remote possibility of data security violations. In the event of such occurrences, the CRA is not responsible for any damages you may experience as a result.

Feedback

We would be glad to receive your feedback if you have suggestions about how we can improve GST/HST Internet File Transfer or if you have had problems using the service.

If you do email us, do not share personal information. Except in rare cases, we do not reply to incoming e-mails. In particular, we will not respond to questions about your own or your business's income tax situation. For questions about taxes, please contact the appropriate CRA office.

Also, do not use this email address to ask for a change to your mailing address. Instead, see How to change your address.

Do not send confidential information - such as your access code - via Internet e-mail. Our encryption system does not function on email.
